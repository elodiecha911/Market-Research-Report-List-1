<p><p>The colloids market for blood plasma is divided into two primary types: natural and synthetic. Natural colloids, derived from biological sources like albumin, are used for volume expansion and maintaining oncotic pressure. Synthetic colloids, such as hydroxyethyl starch or dextrans, are engineered to mimic the properties of natural plasma while offering advantages like longer circulation times and predictable effects. Both types play crucial roles in medical treatments, particularly in surgeries and trauma care, ensuring effective fluid management.</p></p>
