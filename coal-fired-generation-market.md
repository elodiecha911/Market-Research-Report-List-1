<p><p>Coal-fired generation markets primarily consist of Pulverized Coal Combustion (PCC) and Cyclone Furnace systems. PCC is characterized by finely ground coal burned in a furnace, allowing for efficient heat transfer and high electricity output. In contrast, Cyclone Furnaces utilize larger coal particles, creating a swirling motion to enhance combustion efficiency and reduce emissions. Both systems serve to produce electricity, but they differ in fuel processing, combustion techniques, and efficiency, influencing their adoption based on operational needs and environmental regulations.</p></p>
