<p><p>The termite treatment market comprises two primary types: non-chemical and chemical treatments. Non-chemical treatments include physical barriers, biological agents, and heat treatment, which focus on prevention and extermination without harmful substances. In contrast, chemical treatments involve insecticides and pesticides to eliminate termites effectively. These chemicals can be applied through soil application, baiting systems, or direct spraying. Each approach offers distinct advantages, catering to varying preferences for safety, effectiveness, and environmental impact in termite control.</p></p>
